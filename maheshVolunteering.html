One Plant Many Families

I volunteered with OPMF(One Plant Many Families) all four years of high school( serving as a leader junior and senior year). We strive to serve out community, one plant at a time; with our main goal being to alleviate the drastic food shortages in our communities. We grow our own fresh produce while simultaneously collecting donations from willing neighbours/markets to donate to the ‘Food for the Journey Project’(a local group that services the Dayton community).

Youth Group For Indian Culture

I have volunteered with YGIC(Youth Group For Indian Culture) for almost two years now. As a volunteer, I participated in creating winter clothing to be donated to the local food pantry. It was here that I learned of the large population of Daytonians that live without adequate food and shelter, along with the steps I could take to change this. In the end, we donated over 50 handmade blankets and ponchos; having volunteered over 50 hours each.

Washington-Centerville Teen Advisory Board

I have volunteered with TAB(the Washington-Centerville Teen Advisory Board) for almost two years now. TAB is a group of innovative and energetic teens in Grades 6 to 12, who help shape the future of the Library by developing programs and services, recommending materials for purchase, and promoting the Library to other teens. TAB meets twice a month and has a blast creating new ways to make the Library "teen friendly."
